**BioOlympia** is an Olympia, Washington based grassroots [Bio]Punk hacker space and life sciences project. It focuses on 
hands-on, community-driven biology, enabling individuals to engage in scientific exploration—such as genetic engineering and 
tissue culture—outside the constraints of traditional, corporate institutions. 

**Core Activities & Initiatives**
**Community Science:** The lab provides an open environment where the citizen scientists and biohackers can perform standard 
laboratory procedures. Projects range from basic cell culture and stem cell research to maintaining essential equipment like
incubators and pipettes.

**Events & Fundraising:** Because it operates as a grassroots initiative, BioOlympia regularly partners with the local Pacific
Northwest arts and music scene (including bands like [Electric Sweats](https://electricsweats.net)) to host benefit shows, swap 
meets, and lab workshops.

**Leadership:** The lab's operations are driven by local scientists Matthew Woslum and Zee Zinck.

**How to Get Involved:** Because BioOlympia operates on a do-it-yourself (DIY) and open-science philosophy, the project welcomes 
engagement from curious learners and fellow biohackers. Feel free to reach out!

**Support:** The project relies heavily on community backing and crowdfunding to maintain operations and acquire lab supplies. You 
can view their ongoing fundraising efforts via their [GoFundMe Campaign](https://www.gofundme.com/f/support-bio-olympias-stem-cell-research). 

**Network:** For wider discussions on the DIY biology movement, you can connect with similar grassroots scientists via the Google Groups 
DIYbio Forum.

**Meet the Team:** To track their latest experiments, lab upgrades, and community events, follow [BioOlympia on Instagram](https://www.gofundme.com/f/support-bio-olympias-stem-cell-research).
