**BioOlympia** is an Olympia, Washington based grassroots [Bio]Punk hacker space and life sciences project. It focuses on 
hands-on, community-driven biology, enabling individuals to engage in scientific exploration—such as genetic engineering and 
tissue culturing—outside the constraints of traditional, corporate institutions. 


**Core Activities & Initiatives**

-Community Science: The lab provides an open environment where citizen scientists and biohackers can perform standard 
laboratory procedures. Projects range from basic cell culture and stem cell research to bioinformatics research.

-Events & Fundraising: Because it operates as a grassroots initiative, BioOlympia regularly partners with the local Pacific
Northwest arts and music scene (including bands like [Electric Sweats](https://electricsweats.net)) to host benefit shows, swap 
meets, and lab workshops.

-Networking: Because the lab operates on a do-it-yourself (DIY) and open-science philosophy, BioOlympia welcomes engagement from curious learners and fellow biohackers. Feel free to reach out!

For wider discussions on the DIY biology movement, you can connect with similar grassroots scientists via the Google Groups 
DIYbio Forum.
